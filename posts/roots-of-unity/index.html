Roots of Unity

Mar 14, 2023

The nnnth roots of unity are the solutions to the equation

xn−1=0x^n-1=0xn−1=0

And the fundamental theorem of algebra tells us there are exactly nnn solutions. Evidently 1 is always a solution and -1 is a solution only if nnn is even. To find all the solutions, including the complex ones we do a bit of algebra to get

xn−1=0xn=1x=1n\begin{align*} x^n-1 & =0 \\ x^n & =1 \\ x & =\sqrt[n]{1} \\ \end{align*}xn−1xnx​=0=1=n1​​

And to find the complex nnnth roots of 1 we need to think of a complex number a+bia+bia+bi as a point on a plane where the real number aaa is on the x-axis and the imaginary number bbb is on the y-axis. Then we can represent a complex number in polar form r(cos⁡θ+isin⁡θ)r(\cos \theta + i\sin \theta)r(cosθ+isinθ) where rrr is the distance the point is from the origin and θ\thetaθ is the angle.

We actually have infinitely many ways to represent the complex number as

r(cos⁡(θ+2πk)+isin⁡(θ+2πk)) for k∈Zr(\cos(\theta + 2\pi k) + i\sin(\theta + 2\pi k)) \text{ for } k\in \mathbb{Z}r(cos(θ+2πk)+isin(θ+2πk)) for k∈Z

Because turning an angle by 360 degrees (or equivalently 2π2\pi2π radians) doesn't change it. Also, by Euler's formula

r(cos⁡(θ+2πk)+isin⁡(θ+2πk))=rei(θ+2πk)r(\cos(\theta + 2\pi k) + i\sin(\theta + 2\pi k))=re^{i(\theta+2\pi k)}r(cos(θ+2πk)+isin(θ+2πk))=rei(θ+2πk)

And expressing a complex number as a power of eee makes it really easy to take the nnnth root of a complex number as we just represent it in polar form and raise it to the 1/nnnth power

(rei(θ+2πk))1/n=r1/nei(θ+2πk)/n for k=0,1,2,…,n−1(re^{i(\theta + 2\pi k)})^{1/n}=r^{1/n}e^{i(\theta + 2\pi k)/n} \text{ for } k=0,1,2,\dotsc,n-1(rei(θ+2πk))1/n=r1/nei(θ+2πk)/n for k=0,1,2,…,n−1

Finding the nnnth root of unity becomes

xn−1=0x=1n=1ei0n=11/nei(0+2πk)/n for k=0,1,2,…,n−1=ei(2πk)/n for k=0,1,2,…,n−1\begin{align*} x^n-1 & =0 \\ x & =\sqrt[n]{1} \\ & =\sqrt[n]{1e^{i0}} \\ & =1^{1/n}e^{i(0 + 2\pi k)/n} & \text{ for } k=0,1,2,\dotsc,n-1 \\ & =e^{i(2\pi k)/n} & \text{ for } k=0,1,2,\dotsc,n-1 \end{align*}xn−1x​=0=n1​=n1ei0​=11/nei(0+2πk)/n=ei(2πk)/n​ for k=0,1,2,…,n−1 for k=0,1,2,…,n−1​
